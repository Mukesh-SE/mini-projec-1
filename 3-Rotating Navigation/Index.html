Five Dimensions and Principles of Interaction Design

Interaction Design has five dimensions, each standing for a different IxD aspect that designers pay attention to when deciding on their design. Let’s briefly go over them:

Five Dimensions of Interaction Design

1D: Words

The first dimension is about words that are used in the interaction. exts you see on CTA buttons (e.g. download, add to cart, sign up) can be examples of such words.

2D: Visual Representations

The second dimension is related to the visual sphere. Typography, images, or icons can be examples of visual representations.

3D: Physical Objects or Space

The third dimension is about the object users utilize to interact with the product, as well as the environment that interaction takes place in.

4D: Time on Session

The fourth dimension measures the amount of time you spend during an interaction.

5D: Behavior

he last dimension concerns how you, as a user, interact with a product. It’s a combination of all previous dimensions and your emotional response towards the interaction.
